Dependance Ardos has always been dependent on someone throughout his life. Though this person has changed, it’s always been someone. First it was his mother, as do most children as they grow and mature. Then it was his followers, who watched him grow and would help mold him into the god he was meant to be. Then it was Koara, who helped him realize that he didn’t have to be who everyone wanted him to be. Then it was Koara’s statue, and the countless paintings he would paint of his partner. Even though a person wasn’t there in the normal sense, Koara remained, forever stuck in stone. For a while it was the goddess Persephone. The same god who blessed him when he was born, the same goddess he turned to when he understood that he was a god himself. There was always someone he depended on weather he realized it or not. Without these people, he wouldn’t be himself, he wouldn’t be the same man who could be recognized throughout time. Who was written about in some of the most important texts throughout history. The same man who is willing to do anything necessary to bring his partner back. He’d be normal. He’d live out his life in the same city he was born in, find a wife, have a few kids, and then pass during the Roman war or even in his sleep. He’d be nothing.
